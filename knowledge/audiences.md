# The Four Audiences

Every piece targets ONE primary audience. Cross-post adaptations may shift the frame, but a piece written for everyone reaches no one.

## 1. AI Developers
**Who:** Engineers shipping with LLMs daily. Live on HN, X (tech Twitter), GitHub, Discord, r/LocalLLaMA, r/MachineLearning.
**What earns their attention:** Specificity, receipts, real failure stories, benchmarks they can reproduce, novel workflow patterns. They smell marketing instantly and punish it publicly.
**What dies:** Hype, "AI will change everything," anything that reads like a LinkedIn post, unverifiable claims.
**Mitchell's edge with them:** He builds in public with a real multi-agent production system (career-ops). Show the harness, the bug classes, the spend ledgers. "Agent babysitting," "context windows and drift," "what's a harness" are native to this audience.
**Register:** Peer, not teacher. Show code/configs. Admit tradeoffs unprompted.

## 2. AI Builders
**Who:** Technical-adjacent makers: indie hackers, PMs who ship, designers using Cursor/Claude Code, ops people automating. Live on X, LinkedIn, YouTube, Substack, Discord communities.
**What earns their attention:** "Here's exactly how I did it" with steps they can copy. Cost transparency ($X to build Y). Before/after workflow comparisons.
**What dies:** Pure theory, gatekeeping jargon without a bridge.
**Mitchell's edge:** He is the archetype: non-traditional path into building. "Skills, MCPs, tools oh my," "memory files," "making AI give you what you want" are for them.
**Register:** Generous senior peer who remembers being confused.

## 3. Newly AI-Enabled
**Who:** Professionals just discovering what AI can do for their work: teachers, tradespeople, small business owners, mid-career folks. Live on TikTok, YouTube, Facebook, LinkedIn, general Reddit.
**What earns their attention:** One concrete problem solved on screen, money/time saved in dollars and hours, "you don't need to be technical" proof, warmth.
**What dies:** Jargon, dev-culture in-jokes, anything requiring a terminal in the first 60 seconds.
**Mitchell's edge:** Journalist's translation instinct + genuine democratization conviction. "AI for blue collar workers," "change the law with AI," "live consult with a family member" are made for them.
**Register:** The friend who happens to know computers. Never condescending. Show, don't lecture.

## 4. General Audience
**Who:** Everyone thinking about AI's effect on society, kids, work, environment. All platforms; Substack essays + TikTok/YT for reach.
**What earns their attention:** Stakes framed through their life (their kid, their job, their power bill), honest complexity without both-sidesing, a clear "so what should I do."
**What dies:** Doomer/booster binaries, insider debates, wonkery without a human anchor.
**Mitchell's edge:** Emmy-nominated storyteller. This is the newsroom skillset applied to the AI beat. "Parenting in the age of AI," "data centers," "exec disconnect," "18+ AI" live here.
**Register:** Broadcast journalist: hook, human story, evidence, takeaway.

## Cross-audience mechanics
- A dev-audience piece can be atomized DOWN (dev essay → builder thread → enabled-audience TikTok explaining one concept). Rarely works upward.
- The four audiences share ONE thing: they distrust polish. Mitchell's authenticity register (production war stories, real numbers, admitted mistakes) travels across all four.
