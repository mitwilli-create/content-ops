# TikTok Playbook

**Role in the system:** Audience-3 reach engine. Where "newly enabled" and general audiences actually discover AI help. Mitchell's broadcast/AJ+ background is a structural advantage: AJ+ vertical-video grammar basically invented this format.

## Format that works
- 30-60s primary; 90s+ only when watch-through is proven.
- Hook in first 1.5 seconds, visual + spoken ("Your electrician can use AI better than most programmers. Here's how.").
- Screen-recording + face bubble for "watch me solve this" content; direct-to-camera for takes.
- Captions always on (majority watch muted); on-screen text reinforces the one takeaway.
- One idea per video. The essay's 8 points = 8 videos, not 1.
- End with a loop or a "part 2" hook; completions + rewatches outrank likes.

## Timing & cadence (BASELINE Jan 2026 — /timing-check; TikTok meta shifts fastest of all 9)
- Consistency (3-5x/week) matters more than clock timing early; algorithm tests each video independently of follower count.
- Evening + lunch windows local-US historically strongest for how-to content.
- First 200-500 views decide the test cohort: strong completion rate → next tier.

## Series from the ledger
- "AI for the rest of us" — #8 blue collar, #9 change the law, #10 making AI work for you, #18 live family consults (STRONGEST format-fit in the whole ledger: real person, real anxiety, real solve, episodic)
- "Parent mode" — #6, #7, #12 (YT-not-AI is a perfect contrarian hook)
- "Field producer secrets" — #13 multimodal for shoots (his credential is the hook)

## Guardrails
- Never post autonomously; agent scripts + storyboards, Mitchell shoots and posts.
- AI-content fatigue is real: lead with the human problem, mention AI second.
- Reply-with-video to good comments is the cheapest high-performing format.
