## Algorithm (as of 2026-03, platform official)
- LinkedIn replaced its ranking stack with a unified LLM-based Generative Recommender: your last ~1,000 interactions are ranked as a sequence, weighting dwell time, profile relevance, and topical "expertise DNA" over network size or vanity engagement (LinkedIn Engineering Blog, 2026-03). Practical read: consistent topical depth beats one-off virality.
- Comment quality outweighs reactions: 15+ word comments carry ~2.5x weight, generic "Great post!" replies are discounted (creator-reported, as of 2026-05).
- The 2026 ranking stack is reported as "360Brew", a unified AI ranker that replaced the legacy content infrastructure; dwell time now outranks surface engagement, a post read for ~30s beating one with 50 quick likes (SEO-blog/creator-reported, as of 2026-07; the architecture itself is platform-official per the 2026-03 post above, the 360Brew name and dwell figures are not).

## Format that works
- 150-300 words. Hook line MUST work truncated (~140-210 chars before "...see more"; reconfirmed 2026-06). Note: a 372K-post analysis found peak engagement at ~230-450 words, so the 380-word ceiling is worth an A/B against his own performance log before widening.
- Structure: one-line hook → line break → 3-6 short paragraphs (1-2 sentences each) → concrete takeaway → soft question.
- Native > links: external links are penalized (third-party estimates vary 19-60%, no official number, as of 2026-03; RE-CONFIRMED 2026-07-22, with sources now converging near ~60% less reach). The link-in-first-comment workaround is now reportedly detected and suppressed too (re-confirmed 2026-07-22, "penalized as of early 2026"). Prefer full-value native excerpt with "full essay on my Substack" as plain text (SEO-blog reported, as of 2026-07).
- Document posts (PDF carousels) still the top format: ~6.1-6.6% engagement benchmark, optimal 7-10 slides (creator-reported data, as of 2026-06).
- Native video: 60s-3min performs best (dwell time rewarded); burned-in captions are mandatory, majority watch muted (creator-reported, as of 2026-06).

## Timing (verified 2026-07-06, /timing-check before scheduling)
- Tue-Thu 10am-noon is the current strongest baseline window, with a NEW 3-8pm post-work window rivaling mornings ("Wednesday 4pm" tops Buffer's 4.8M-post dataset); video peaks 12-2pm and 5-7pm (aggregated third-party data, as of 2026-06). Mitchell should test an afternoon PT slot, not just ~8am PT.
- First 30-60 min of engagement decide distribution (narrower than the old 60-90 min claim; only ~5% of posts that underperform hour 1 recover). Post when he can reply substantively to every comment immediately.
- 3-5 posts/week with a minimum 18-24h gap between posts; two posts within ~4h cannibalize each other (creator-reported data, as of 2026-06).

## Engagement mechanics
- Reply to EVERY comment in the first hour, substantively (15+ words), not just fast.
- 15 min of thoughtful comments on others' posts before AND after posting warms distribution (reconfirmed 2026-06).
- Tag no one unless they genuinely contributed; tag-spam reads desperate.
- HASHTAGS (refreshed 2026-07-22, SOURCES CONFLICT, no platform-official guidance): hashtag-marketing blogs claim 3-5 relevant tags still give a small lift (figures range from "2-5% reach" to "30% more engagement", all vendor-adjacent and unverifiable); algorithm-analysis sources say hashtags are now at best a weak SEO/semantic signal and one states flatly they are irrelevant. Both camps agree on two things: their impact collapsed from the 2019-2021 peak, and volume is actively harmful (20-30 tags trips spam detection and throttles reach). Practical rule: 0-3 max, only genuinely topical ones, never as a discovery strategy. Treat any specific percentage here as folklore.
- Polls are reported dead as a reach tactic in 2026 (SEO-blog, as of 2026-07); one source still lists a Tue-Wed 10am-noon poll window, so treat polls as unresolved and not worth Mitchell's slot either way.
