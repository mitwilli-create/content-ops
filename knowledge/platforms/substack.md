**Role in the system:** Home base. Every pillar idea becomes a Substack essay FIRST; all other platforms atomize from it. Owns the audience relationship (email list = the only platform-proof asset).

## Format that works
- 1,200-2,500 words for essays; 600-900 for "field notes" between pillars. (Market data as of 2026-07 puts top-performer averages ~870 words, creator-reported; his range brackets it, no change.)
- Title = the claim, subtitle = the stakes. Assertion titles beat question titles for growth: a 94K-post analysis found question titles cut subscriber conversion ~35%, though they pull more comments per reaction — use a question title only when the goal is discussion, not conversion (creator-reported, as of 2026-07).
- First 2 paragraphs visible in email preview decide open-to-read conversion. Open with the scene or the number, never throat-clearing.
- Section headers every 300-400 words; one image/diagram per 500 words for dev content (architecture sketches outperform stock anything).
- End with ONE question to drive comments; comments and restacks now explicitly outrank hearts in ranking signals (creator-reported, as of 2026-07).
- Native A/B testing for headlines now exists — use it on every pillar essay (platform official, as of 2026-07).
