# Substack Playbook

**Role in the system:** Home base. Every pillar idea becomes a Substack essay FIRST; all other platforms atomize from it. Owns the audience relationship (email list = the only platform-proof asset).

## Format that works
- 1,200-2,500 words for essays; 600-900 for "field notes" between pillars.
- Title = the claim, subtitle = the stakes. Titles as questions underperform titles as assertions for this niche.
- First 2 paragraphs visible in email preview decide open-to-read conversion. Open with the scene or the number, never throat-clearing.
- Section headers every 300-400 words; one image/diagram per 500 words for dev content (architecture sketches outperform stock anything).
- End with ONE question to drive comments (comments boost Substack network distribution).

## Cadence & timing (BASELINE, dated Jan 2026 — run /timing-check before scheduling)
- Weekly cadence beats daily for essay Substacks; consistency beats frequency.
- Tue-Thu mornings ET historically strongest for professional/tech newsletters.
- Notes (Substack's feed) 3-5x/week rebroadcasts essay ideas + drives discovery; Notes is the fastest-changing surface, always T2-check current meta.

## Growth mechanics
- Recommendations from adjacent Substacks are the #1 growth lever: identify 10-15 AI-adjacent Substacks, engage genuinely in their comments for weeks BEFORE asking anything.
- Cross-post 48-72h after publish: LinkedIn (excerpt + link), X (thread of core argument), HN (only if genuinely dev-substantive).
- Free tier only until ~1,000 subs; paywall too early caps growth.
